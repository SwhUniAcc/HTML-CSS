The Classic Margherita Pizza

The Classic Margherita Pizza is a simple yet iconic Italian dish made with a thin, crisp crust topped with rich tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella cheese, and fragrant basil leaves. Drizzled with a touch of olive oil, it perfectly balances flavors of sweetness, creaminess, and freshness.

Ingredients

Dough

F2¼ tsp (1 packet) active dry yeast - ¾ cup warm water (about 110°F / 43°C) - 2 cups all-purpose flour - 1 tsp sugar - 1 tsp salt - 1 tbsp olive oil

Sauce

1 tbsp olive oil - 2 cloves garlic, minced - 1 can (14 oz / 400g) crushed tomatoes - 1 tsp dried oregano - Salt and pepper to taste - Fresh basil leaves (optional)

Toppings

8 oz (225g) fresh mozzarella, sliced - Fresh basil leaves - Olive oil for drizzling

Instructions

Dough

In a bowl, dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water. Let it sit for 5–10 minutes until frothy. Then, add flour, salt, and olive oil. Mix until a dough forms. After that, begin to knead the dough on a floured surface for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Finally, place in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let rise for 1 hour (until doubled in size).

Sauce

Heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat, Add garlic and sauté then wait for 30 seconds before adding anything else. Then, add crushed tomatoes, oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 10–15 minutes. (Optionally, stir in a few torn basil leaves for extra flavor)

Assemble the Pizza

you must first preheat oven to 475°F (245°C). Then, punch down dough and divide into two balls. Roll each into a 12-inch circle and then place on a baking sheet or pizza stone. Then you must spread sauce evenly over the dough. Finally, top with mozzarella slices and fresh basil and drizzle lightly with olive oil.

Bake and Serve

Bake for 12–15 minutes, or until crust is golden and cheese is bubbling. Let the pizza cool for a minute, slice, and enjoy your fresh, homemade pizza!
